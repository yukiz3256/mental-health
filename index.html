What Is Mental Health?
Mental Health is something that includes our emotions, psychology and our social wealth. It is different for everyone, it affects how we think, act and feel, therefore can change how we handle our stress and choices. It's not a easy topic to discuss or bring up about, any can experience mental health problems from child to adulthood. From themselves, to family problems to past life experiences. Most common illnesses are depression, anxiety disorders, schizophrenia, eating disorders, psychotic disorders, and addictive behaviors. You can take a Mental Health Screening at https://screening.mhanational.org/screening-tools/
Symptons
Common symptons are someone feeling helpness, hopeless, getting angry, tired and hungry often, not being able to sleep, not having an appetite, not wanting to talk to family & friends, thinks of harming yourself or others, feeling numb or pain, loosing interest or relationships with anything.
Being Okay
Everyone IS different, and you are not alone. Its okay to feel stressed or exhuasted, do not let one thing takeaway your whole day! And even so, one bad day does not mean all is over. Taking care of yourself should be #1. Reduce your screen time a little bit, staying away from media is actually good for your health. Take some time to find a hobby! Drawing, Baking, Cooking, Do what YOU love. Eat well, don't rely on sugar too much either. Sticking to a good healthy diet can help you have a happier outlook, improve ability to focus and sets yourself up as well! Exercise, as much as you might hate it, go for a walk or a run. It reduces your negative mood and improves self esteem. Real studies have shown exercising reduces the risk of developing mental illness. Talk to someone! You never know, you may feel relief after you let it all out. Lastly, it is absolutely okay to take a break. Taking care of yourself is self care. We can come back with new experiences, more producitivty, motivation, and creativeness.

Me
There should be this app, where specifically it’s for teens that struggle to download. It could be for homework help, ranting, looking for clothes and shoe suggestions, etc. Some of us have a hard time speaking up, about almost anything. So this app is finding new friends that you might not ever even meet. I was thinking with this app, it can have a voice changer option, you can open the app and pair with someone to talk to. Then you and the other person may have a conversation and talk about similar problems or help each other out. With a voice changer and app that only allows teens to download , it’ll make many of us feel more comfortable and not so afraid.
